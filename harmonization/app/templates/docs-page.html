Contour label RTSTRUCT ROI Name normalization and harmonization are vital in medical imaging and radiation therapy. These practices enhance the quality of patient care, streamline treatment planning, and support research efforts, ultimately advancing the field of radiation therapy and medical imaging.

Zip File Format

ROI Name Harmonization is applied based on the existing ROI Name in the RTSTRUCT. It is preferable to create a ZIP file containing the planning CT along with the RTSTRUCT for streamlined data management.
Contour Label Harmonization applications are performed for both single and multiple patient information, and the results are presented in a ZIP format for convenient access and organization of medical data..

Normalization

ROI Name Normalization adheres to specific rules for consistency:

Step 1 Converts all characters to lowercase.
Step 2 OTrims leading/trailing spaces.
Step 3 Replaces special characters with spaces.
Step 4 Condenses consecutive spaces/special characters.
Step 5 Converts spaces to underscores, except the trailing one.
Step 6 Capitalizes the first letter after each space. These steps ensure uniform, clean, and structured.
Example:
# ROI Name Normalised Name
1 Right Femur Right_Femur
2 RIGHT FEMUR Right_Femur
1 Right_Femur Right_Femur

TG263 Nomenclature

TG-263 is a nomenclature and naming standardization effort in radiation oncology. It's primarily focused on standardizing the naming and labeling of structures in radiotherapy and medical imaging, particularly within the context of DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine) files and RT (Radiation Therapy) datasets.
The aim of TG-263 is to establish consistent and universally understood naming conventions for anatomical structures, regions of interest (ROIs), and contours used in treatment planning, dose calculations, and medical image analysis. This standardization enhances patient safety, facilitates data exchange among different healthcare systems, and promotes uniformity in research and clinical practice in the field of radiation oncology. It's important for reducing errors, improving interoperability, and advancing the quality of patient care in radiotherapy.
